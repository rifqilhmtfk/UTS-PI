The Contested Al Aqsa Mosque

The Al Aqsa Mosque is the entire Al-Haram Asy-Syarif complex, Al-Quds, covering an area of 14 hectares. Inside the complex there is Jami' Al Aqsa with a silver dome, and the Dome of The Rock Qubbah Shakhra' in the form of a golden dome which is believed to be the place where the Prophet Muhammad SAW set foot on his Isra Mikraj journey. The Dome of The Rock Mosque is often confused with the Al-Aqsa Mosque.

Battle of Badr

The Battle of Badr (Arabic: غزوة بدر, translit. gazwah badr), was the first major battle between Muslims and their enemies. It took place on 17 Ramadan 2 AH (March 13, 625). A small Muslim force of 313 men fought the 1,000-strong Quraysh army from Mecca. After fighting fiercely for about two hours, the Muslims destroyed the Quraysh army's defensive line, which then retreated in disarray.
